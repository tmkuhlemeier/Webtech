Forward and Reverse Caching

Instead of classifying caches into browser and proxy caches you can also classify as a forward cache or a reverse cache. These classifications are dependent on the location of the cache. A forward cache is a cache which is located outside the network of a webserver. These are located in the local network of the visitor, the network of an institute/company or the network of an ISP. Examples of this are the internal memory of the host or an institutional proxy cache. Forward caches are physically located closer to the host than reverse caches, which means the propagation delay will be smaller when requesting something from the forward cache. This means that less routers will have to be traversed and the queuing delay will be smaller. The other type of cache that can be classified is the reverse cache. This cache is present in the CDN (Content Delivery Network) and it can also be called a gateway proxy. [1]
